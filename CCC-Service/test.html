Primary Care

Covenant Community Care offers affordable, quality primary health care for the whole family. Most patients without health insurance pay between $20 and $50 per visit, depending on family size and income. The cost of the visit covers:

  • Visit with Primary Care Physician (PCP)
  • Blood draw and lab work
  • Required Adult Replacement Vaccines that you qualify for
  • Diabetes and other health care issue education.

We never want price to be a barrier to receiving necessary medical attention. If you feel that you are unable to pay the cost of a visit, please call your preferred clinic to schedule an appointment with a Benefits Counselor to discuss your situation.

Pedatric Care

Covenant Community Care offers pediatric care at all of our medical clinic locations. We believe that ALL children deserve good health, and we are committed to providing a safe and nurturing medical home for your child. We know you don’t want to wait for an appointment when your child is sick, so in addition to scheduled well visit appointments, same-day sick visit and walk-in appointment slots have been reserved for our pediatric clients.

We accept all Medicaid and MiChild plans. If your child is not insured our Benefits Counselors are always available to screen him or her for Medicaid eligibility.

We look forward to seeing you and your child!

Centering Pregnancy (Obstetrics)

Our goal is to provide quality prenatal care to all mothers. We are always accepting new patients, regardless of insurance status. At Covenant we offer Centering Pregnancy©.

Centering Pregnancy replaces traditional one-on-one visits with group visits and brings 8-12 women who are due at the same time, together for prenatal care. At each visit after health assessments are complete, the provider and support staff ‘circle-up’ with moms and support people.

They facilitate discussion and interactive activities designed to address important and timely health topics while leaving room to discuss what is important to the group. Centering materials help moms and providers ensure that everything from nutrition, common discomforts, stress management, labor and delivery, breastfeeding, and infant care are covered in group.

The Centering Pregnancy model has proven results: according to CHI 96% of women surveyed prefer receiving their prenatal care in a Centering group, 33% of women are less likely to have a preterm baby, and women are shown to have high rates of breast feeding.
